India's diverse climate zones require tomato varieties suited to different seasons and temperatures. Here are some recommended tomato breeds for different seasons and temperature ranges in India:

1. Summer (Hot and Dry Climates):

Roma VF: Ideal for hot and dry climates. Produces pear-shaped fruits, suitable for paste and sauce.
Pusa Ruby: Heat-tolerant variety with good disease resistance, suitable for hotter regions.
Bangalore Tomato*: Well-adapted to high temperatures and can thrive in hot climates.

2. Monsoon and Cooler Regions:

Arka Rakshak: Resistant to multiple diseases and adaptable to varying temperatures, suitable for monsoon regions.
Pusa Hybrid: Versatile hybrid with good disease resistance, suitable for changing climates during monsoon.
Brandywine: Heirloom variety with good flavor, performs decently in cooler temperatures.

3. Year-Round Cultivation:

Bangalore Tomato: Adaptable to various temperatures, making it suitable for year-round cultivation in some regions.
Pusa Hybrid: Known for versatility and adaptability to different climates, making it a good choice throughout the year.
Arka Rakshak: With disease resistance, it can be cultivated almost year-round.

4. High Temperature Zones:

Bangalore Tomato*: Tolerant to high temperatures and well-suited for hot climates.
Pusa Ruby*: Another variety known for its heat tolerance.

5. Lower Temperature Zones:

Brandywine: Performs reasonably well in cooler temperatures and can be considered for regions with milder weather.
Rutgers or Early Girl: These varieties are suitable for areas with slightly lower temperatures.

Always consider local conditions and consult with local nurseries, agricultural experts, or extension services for the most suitable tomato varieties for your specific region within India. Temperature, humidity, and soil conditions can vary significantly, impacting the performance of different tomato breeds.
