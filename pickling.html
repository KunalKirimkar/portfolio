Pickling is a preservation method that involves immersing food in a solution of vinegar, salt, sugar, and spices, which creates an acidic environment that inhibits the growth of spoilage-causing bacteria. Pickled tomatoes offer a tangy flavor and can be enjoyed as a condiment or added to salads, sandwiches, or antipasto platters. Here's how to pickle tomatoes:

1. Select Fresh Tomatoes:

Choose firm, ripe tomatoes with no signs of spoilage or bruising. Cherry or grape tomatoes are often preferred for pickling due to their small size and firm texture.

2. Prepare Brine Solution:

  • In a large non-reactive pot, combine vinegar, water, salt, sugar, and pickling spices (such as mustard seeds, peppercorns, coriander seeds, dill seeds, or garlic cloves).
  • Bring the brine mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the salt and sugar are completely dissolved.

3. Prepare Tomatoes:

  • Wash the tomatoes thoroughly under cold running water to remove any dirt or debris.
  • Prick each tomato with a toothpick or skewer to allow the brine to penetrate the skin and prevent bursting during pickling.

4. Pack Tomatoes in Jars:

  • Pack the prepared tomatoes tightly into clean, sterilized canning jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace at the top.
  • Add fresh herbs, spices, or garlic cloves to the jars for additional flavor if desired.

5. Pour Hot Brine Over Tomatoes:

  • Carefully pour the hot brine over the tomatoes in the jars, covering them completely and leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top.
  • Use a clean utensil to remove any air bubbles trapped in the jars by running it along the inside edge of the jar.

6. Seal Jars:

  • Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue or spills.
  • Place sterilized lids and bands on the jars and tighten them fingertip-tight.

7. Process Jars (Optional):

If desired, process the jars in a boiling water bath canner for additional shelf stability. Process pint jars for 10-15 minutes and quart jars for 15-20 minutes.

8. Cool and Store:

  • Once processed (if applicable), remove the jars from the canner and let them cool completely at room temperature.
  • Check the seals by pressing down on the center of each lid. If the lid does not flex or pop, it is sealed.
  • Label sealed jars with the contents and date, then store them in a cool, dark place for at least 2 weeks before opening to allow the flavors to develop.

9. Usage:

Pickled tomatoes can be enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or condiment. They pair well with cheeses, charcuterie, salads, sandwiches, and burgers. They can also be chopped and added to relishes, salsas, or chutneys for added flavor.


By following these steps, you can easily pickle tomatoes at home and enjoy their tangy flavor and versatility in various dishes. Adjust the seasonings and spices to suit your taste preferences and experiment with different varieties of tomatoes for unique flavor profiles.
