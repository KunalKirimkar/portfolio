Drying, also known as dehydration, is a preservation method that removes moisture from tomatoes, extending their shelf life and concentrating their flavor. Here's how to dry tomatoes:

1. Select Ripe Tomatoes:

Choose ripe, firm tomatoes with no signs of spoilage or bruising. Roma or plum tomatoes are often preferred for drying due to their meaty texture and low moisture content.

2. Wash and Prepare Tomatoes:

  • Wash the tomatoes thoroughly under cold running water to remove any dirt or debris.
  • Remove stems and any blemishes or bruises from the tomatoes.
  • Optionally, you can cut the tomatoes in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds and pulp to reduce drying time and improve texture.

3. Preheat the Oven or Dehydrator:

  • If you're using a food dehydrator, follow the manufacturer's instructions for preheating and operating the unit.

4. Slice Tomatoes:

Slice the tomatoes into uniform thickness, typically around 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick. Thicker slices will take longer to dry.

5. Optional Pre-Treatment:

To prevent browning and preserve color, you can pre-treat the tomato slices in a solution of lemon juice or vinegar (1 tablespoon per quart of water) for 10-15 minutes. Alternatively, you can dip them in a solution of sulfite or ascorbic acid.

6. Arrange Tomatoes for Drying:

Place the tomato slices in a single layer on drying trays or baking sheets lined with parchment paper or silicone mats. Make sure the slices are not touching or overlapping to ensure even drying.

7. Drying Process:

  • Place the trays in the preheated oven or food dehydrator.
  • Dry the tomatoes until they are leathery and slightly pliable, but not brittle. This usually takes about 6 to 12 hours, depending on the thickness of the slices, humidity levels, and the drying method used.
  • Rotate the trays occasionally for even drying, especially if using an oven where heat distribution may not be uniform.

8. Cool and Store:

  • Once the tomatoes are fully dried, remove them from the oven or dehydrator and let them cool completely at room temperature.
  • Store the dried tomatoes in airtight containers or resealable bags in a cool, dark place. They can also be stored in the refrigerator or freezer for longer shelf life.

9. Usage:

Rehydrate dried tomatoes by soaking them in warm water, broth, or olive oil for about 15-30 minutes before using them in recipes. Alternatively, you can add them directly to soups, stews, sauces, salads, or pasta dishes for an intense burst of flavor.


By following these steps, you can easily dry tomatoes at home to enjoy their concentrated flavor and versatility in various culinary applications.
